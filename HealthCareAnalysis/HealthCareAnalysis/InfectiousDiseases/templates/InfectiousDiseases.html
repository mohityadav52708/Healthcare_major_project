Infectious Diseases in India: A Persistent Challenge

Infectious diseases continue to be a major public health concern in India, with diseases like tuberculosis, malaria, dengue, and diarrheal diseases affecting millions every year. Key Drivers: Overcrowding, poor sanitation, and limited access to clean water and healthcare facilities. Health Impact: These diseases cause significant morbidity and mortality, especially among vulnerable populations. Government efforts focus on vaccination, sanitation, and public health campaigns, but improving healthcare access and addressing social determinants are critical to combating infectious diseases.
