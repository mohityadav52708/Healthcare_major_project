Click on a disease to learn more

Covid

A contagious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, affecting respiratory health.

Symptoms include fever, cough, and difficulty breathing.
Cardiovascular Diseases

A group of disorders affecting the heart and blood vessels, leading to heart attack and stroke.

Risk factors include hypertension, diabetes, and smoking.
Cancer

A disease where abnormal cells divide uncontrollably and destroy body tissue.

Early detection through screening can significantly improve outcomes.
Chronic Respiratory Diseases

Diseases like asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) affecting breathing.

Avoiding tobacco smoke is crucial for prevention.
Diabetes

A metabolic disease that causes high blood sugar, leading to serious health issues.

Managing diet and exercise is vital for control.
HIV/AIDS

A virus that attacks the immune system, leading to life-threatening infections.

Regular testing and early treatment can prolong life.
Hypertension

High blood pressure that can lead to heart disease and stroke.

Lifestyle changes can help manage blood pressure effectively.
Infectious Diseases

Illnesses caused by pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, or parasites.

Vaccination and hygiene are key prevention strategies.
Kidney Diseases

Conditions that affect the function of the kidneys, potentially leading to failure.

Staying hydrated and managing blood pressure can help.
Stroke

A medical emergency where blood flow to the brain is interrupted.

Acting fast is crucial for minimizing damage.
